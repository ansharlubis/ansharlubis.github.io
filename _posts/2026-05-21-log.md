School is great but now everything I write have to be top-down. Easy to digest. Good diction of course, and no bad grammar. Again, school is great, but now I have to make sure that everything that came out of my mouth are comprehended. Misunderstanding plagues my decision making. I am obsessed with having everyone understands and me understanding what I'm saying. "To be unbound by the rules of literature," to crudely paraphrase a book I'm reading, is but a dream. This education has enriched and has stifled.

And what of imagination, what about the wonderful shape of that cloud? They went buried under relentless desire for syllogistic expressions. The elements not belonging to the domain have to be derivable from the existing elements of the domain, or else they don't exist.

I will not allow myself to psychoanalyse my own shelf, to dig through the misty memory of childhood to come up with the most unscrupulous alibi just so I get to say "That's why," instead of just doing the thing that I want to do. And so those bottles lined up at the top self look like siblings. The tallest in the center looks like the oldest but we all know that the shorter bottle at the end of the line with the black label is their elder.

That is what I desire. To say things without expecting anything in return. No replies. Not even a nod of understanding.
